**TL;DR: If your refund is delayed beyond 21 days, first check the IRS "Where’s My Refund" portal. If the status is "Received" but not "Approved," ensure no notices were mailed to you before contacting a tax professional or the Taxpayer Advocate Service.**

## Practical Steps to Take

1. **Check Status:** Use the [Where's My Refund](https://www.irs.gov/refunds) tool.
2. **Review Notices:** Look for IRS Letter 12C or 4883C.
3. **Request Transcripts:** Sometimes the transcript shows a "Freeze Code" before a letter arrives.

## FAQ

### How long does an IRS refund usually take?
Typically 21 days for electronically filed returns. Paper returns can take 6 months or longer.

### What should I do if my refund is delayed?
Use the IRS "Where's My Refund" tool or request an account transcript to see if there are any holds on your account.
